Medicare Advantage (Part C)

Medicare Advantage plans bundle your hospital, medical, and often prescription drug coverage into one simple plan. Many also include extras like dental, vision, hearing, and even gym memberships — often at $0 premium.

If you're turning 65 or newly eligible for Medicare, this is a great starting point. We'll compare carriers in your area and find the plan that covers your doctors and prescriptions.

  • All-in-one coverage: hospital, medical, and usually prescriptions
  • Often includes dental, vision, and hearing benefits
  • Many plans available at $0 monthly premium
  • Network-based plans with lower out-of-pocket costs
Best for: Seniors turning 65, those newly eligible for Medicare, or anyone wanting all their coverage under one plan.

Medicare Supplements (Medigap)

Original Medicare covers a lot — but not everything. Medigap plans fill in the gaps like copays, coinsurance, and deductibles so you're not hit with unexpected bills.

The biggest advantage? You can see any doctor or specialist nationwide that accepts Medicare. No referrals, no network restrictions. It's the ultimate peace of mind.

  • Covers costs Original Medicare doesn't — copays, coinsurance, deductibles
  • See any doctor nationwide that accepts Medicare
  • No referrals needed for specialists
  • Predictable monthly costs with fewer surprises
Best for: Medicare enrollees who want the freedom to see any doctor nationwide with predictable, minimal out-of-pocket costs.

ACA / Marketplace Plans

Not yet 65? Need coverage for yourself or your family? ACA marketplace plans offer comprehensive health insurance with financial assistance based on your income.

Whether you're self-employed, between jobs, or just need better coverage, we'll help you navigate the marketplace and find a plan you can actually afford.

  • Coverage for individuals and families under 65
  • Financial assistance and subsidies based on income
  • Essential health benefits including maternity and mental health
  • Special enrollment available for qualifying life events
Best for: Individuals and families under 65 — especially self-employed, between jobs, early retirees, or anyone needing comprehensive coverage.

Life insurance isn't just about the unexpected — it's about protecting the people you love. From covering final expenses to leaving a financial cushion for your family, we make it simple to find the right amount of coverage.

Accident & Critical Illness

Life throws curveballs. Accident and critical illness plans give you an extra layer of protection by paying cash benefits directly to you — not the hospital — when you need it most.

Use the money for medical bills, mortgage payments, groceries, or anything else. It's your safety net when the unexpected happens.

  • Cash benefits paid directly to you
  • Covers accidents, heart attacks, strokes, cancer, and more
  • Works alongside your existing health insurance
  • Affordable premiums for valuable peace of mind
Best for: Anyone who wants a financial safety net beyond their regular health insurance — especially active families or those with high-deductible plans.
